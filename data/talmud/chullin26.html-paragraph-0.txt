Chullin 26 - Different Stages of Development Two strands of almonds were cultivated in Israel: one, the "bitter almond," whose fruit became bitter as it grew larger; and the other, the "sweet almond," whose fruit became sweeter as it grew larger. What is subject to tithing in the case of bitter almonds (when they are small and sweet) is exempt in the case of sweet almonds (when they are still bitter), and vice versa.
