Niddah 25 - Gravedigger If a woman aborts an embryo full of water, full of blood, or full of multicolored matter, this is not considered birth, and she does not receive the impurity of childbirth. There is no need to suspect that perhaps there was a more fully developed child, but it got disintegrated. However, if the limbs of the baby began to be developed - but the gender cannot as yet be determined - she must observe the overlapping periods of childbirth impurity, then childbirth purity with the stringency of both a female and a male child.
