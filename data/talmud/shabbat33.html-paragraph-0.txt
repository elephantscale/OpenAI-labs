Shabbat 33 - Rewards - and punishments The Talmud lists various behaviors leading to beneficial results. While it is true that individual's rewards may seem incomprehensible, because they are complicated by his previous lives' acts, and by the idea that a reward may be given to a person in this world to repay him for the few good deeds he has done, good acts add to his balance, nation's balance, and the world's balance.
