Nedarim 59 - Vows are bad In trying to answer the question about an onion - whether new growth in it removes the prohibitions of the old one - the Talmud compares it to tithes, to Kohen's portion (terumah), and finally to our original ruling about vows that one takes against a fruit. In this last case, all that grows from that fruit was forbidden, which must prove that new growth does not nullify the old one.
