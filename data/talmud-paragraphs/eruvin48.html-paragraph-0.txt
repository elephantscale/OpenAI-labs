Eruvin 48 - Objects brought to market by non-Jews We know that objects belonging to a Jew have the same limitations on travel as their owner. On Shabbat, one cannot carry at all. But on a Festival, they can be taken only as far as the owner himself can go, two thousand steps from his residence. By contrast, ownerless objects acquire residence on their own, same say, just four steps around them, and others - two thousand steps.
