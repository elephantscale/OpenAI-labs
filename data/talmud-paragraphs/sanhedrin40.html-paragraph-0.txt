Sanhedrin 40 - Examination of Witnesses in Capital Cases They would examine the witnesses with seven questions related to time and place of the incident: which seven year-period did you see the crime, which year, which month, which day of the month, which day of the week, which hour, and in which place. The primary purpose of these questions is to pinpoint the time and place, and allow the possibility of making them disqualified (false) witnesses. In fact, if there is no possibility for the witnesses to be disqualified, their testimony is not accepted.
